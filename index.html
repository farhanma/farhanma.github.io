Mohammed A. Al Farhan
Talks Publications Vita

I am a postdoctoral research associate at the University of Tennessee’s Innovative Computing Laboratory, directed by Professor Jack Dongarra. I am perusing research on developing numerical software libraries for solving linear algebra problems at scale.

I received PhD in computer science, where I was working in the Extreme Computing Research Center (ECRC) at King Abdullah University of Science and Technology (KAUST) with Professor David E. Keyes. My PhD research was centered around optimizing the performance of unstructured grid computations on emerging, energy-austere high performance computing architectures.

Interests -- High Performance Computing (HPC), Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD), Partial Differential Equations (PDE), Unstructured grids, Irregular computations, Sparse linear algebra, Fast Multipole Method (FMM), Structured grids, Thread- and data-level parallelism, Performance engineering of software systems, Parallel and distributed systems, and Computer and system architecture.

I received a master’s in computer science from KAUST, where I spent quite some time working with Professor Mikhail Moshkov on combinatorial machine learning algorithms and applications.

In a previous life, I was a software engineer at Saudi Electricity Company and Saudi Aramco, where I was fortunate to work on various large-scale corporate systems, e.g., DBMS, automations, scheduling, operating systems, and ERP.

Before that, I was an undergraduate at King Faisal University (KFU) – Al-Ahsa, Saudi Arabia.
