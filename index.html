I am a Postdoctoral Researcher in the Extreme Computing Research Center (ECRC) at the King Abdullah University of Science and Technology (KAUST). I work with David E. Keyes on developing algorithms for data-sparse approximations of non-sparse matrices on modren high performance architectures as part of Hierarchical Computations on Manycore Architectures (HiCMA) math library. In addition, I am a Research Consultant in the Innovative Computing Laboratory (ICL) at the University of Tennessee, where I contribute to the development of the Software for Linear Algebra Targeting Exascale (SLATE) math library as part of the world's leading Exascale Computing Project (ECP) of the United States Dpartment of Energey.

Before that, I was a Postdoctoral Researcher at ICL, where I worked with Jack Dongarra on developing algorithms for linear algebra on distributed, multi-core, GPU-accelerated computers as part of SLATE and Matrix Algebra on GPU and Multicore Architectures (MAGMA) math libraries.

I received PHD'19 and MS'13 in Computer Science from KAUST, where I was part of the ECRC working with David E. Keyes on developing performance-centric algorithms for high performance computing, in the context of unstructured grid PDEs and the Fast Multipole Method. My dissertation title is: unstructured computations on emerging architectures.

In a previous life, I was a Software Engineer at Saudi Electricity Company and Saudi Aramco. Before that, I received BS'12 in Computer Science from King Faisal University (KFU).
